What is emotional eating?

Emotional eating is when you use food as a way to cope with feelings. It is a way to soothe, comfort, or numb yourself when feeling overwhelmed, anxious, or stressed. It is often used as a distraction from the painful emotions that we don’t want to feel. This can lead to overeating, binge eating, or a cycle of yo-yo dieting. Emotional eating can also become an addiction, as food can become a substitute for other forms of pleasure or an escape from uncomfortable emotions.

Understanding the psychological triggers behind emotional eating

It is important to understand the underlying psychological triggers that lead to emotional eating. Oftentimes, emotional eating is a result of deeper issues such as low self-esteem or unresolved trauma. It can also be linked to depression, anxiety, or stress. Unhealthy relationships, such as those with food or people, can also be a cause. It is important to get to the root of the issue in order to find a lasting solution.
